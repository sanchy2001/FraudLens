MASTERCARD FRAUD RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY
=======================================
Version 3.2 | Internal Reference Document

SECTION 1: TRANSACTION VOLUME THRESHOLDS
-----------------------------------------
Normal daily transaction volume is defined as the rolling 30-day average
for that day-of-week. A spike is declared when:
  - Volume exceeds 150% of the rolling average (YELLOW alert)
  - Volume exceeds 200% of the rolling average (RED alert)
  - Volume exceeds 300% of the rolling average (CRITICAL alert)

When a CRITICAL alert fires, the Fraud Operations team must be notified
within 30 minutes and an automated investigation must begin immediately.

SECTION 2: HIGH-RISK MERCHANT CATEGORIES
------------------------------------------
The following merchant categories are flagged as HIGH RISK and require
additional scrutiny when transaction volume spikes:

  1. Electronics (MCG 5732, 5734, 5065)
     - Common in card-not-present fraud
     - High average ticket value increases financial exposure
     - Fraudsters often resell electronics for cash

  2. ATM Withdrawals (MCG 6011)
     - Card skimming attacks frequently target ATM networks
     - Sudden ATM spike in a region often indicates a compromised batch
     - Nigerian Prince scams often culminate in ATM withdrawals

  3. Online Retail (MCG 5999)
     - Account takeover fraud most common here
     - Shipping address mismatches are a strong signal

  4. Travel (MCG 4722, 3000-3299)
     - First-party fraud (friendly fraud) is common in travel
     - Unusual country combinations within 24 hours = velocity fraud

SECTION 3: GEOGRAPHIC RISK TIERS
----------------------------------
Countries are classified into three risk tiers based on historical fraud rates:

  TIER 1 (Low Risk): IN, US, GB, DE, AU, SG
  TIER 2 (Medium Risk): BR, AE, CN
  TIER 3 (High Risk): NG, and any country not on the approved list

Transactions from Tier 3 countries that also involve HIGH RISK merchant
categories should be reviewed within 1 hour of detection.

SECTION 4: TIME-OF-DAY PATTERNS
----------------------------------
Historical analysis shows that fraudulent transactions cluster in two windows:
  - Late night  : 01:00 - 04:00 local time (card-not-present fraud peak)
  - Early morning: 06:00 - 08:00 local time (account takeover peak)

A volume spike during these hours is weighted 2x in the risk score.

SECTION 5: VELOCITY RULES
---------------------------
Velocity fraud occurs when a stolen card is used rapidly before the
victim notices. Our rules trigger on:
  - More than 3 transactions from the same device in 10 minutes
  - Transactions in 2+ countries within 4 hours (impossible travel)
  - Amount escalation: each transaction > 150% of the previous one

SECTION 6: INVESTIGATION ESCALATION PROCEDURE
----------------------------------------------
Step 1: Automated detection fires → Investigation Copilot generates report
Step 2: Report reviewed by Level-1 analyst within 1 hour
Step 3: If confirmed fraud pattern → freeze affected accounts, escalate to L2
Step 4: L2 submits SAR (Suspicious Activity Report) within 24 hours
Step 5: Post-incident review within 72 hours to update ML models

SECTION 7: APPROVED RESPONSE ACTIONS
--------------------------------------
The following actions may be taken automatically by the system:
  - SOFT BLOCK    : Flag future transactions for review (no customer impact)
  - HARD BLOCK    : Decline all transactions above $X threshold
  - ACCOUNT FREEZE: Block all activity (requires L2 approval)
  - SMS ALERT     : Notify cardholder of suspicious activity

SECTION 8: FALSE POSITIVE MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------
Blocking legitimate customers is expensive both financially and reputationally.
We maintain a false positive budget of no more than 0.5% of total transactions.
Any model or rule that exceeds this threshold must be reviewed and recalibrated.

If the investigation copilot confidence score is below 70%, the system must
not take automated action. Instead, the report is queued for human review.
This is the core principle of our Responsible AI framework.
